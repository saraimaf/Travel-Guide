France

I spend just over 6 months in the city, Grenoble. This is quite a small city in comparison to many of the other major cities in France, however it made an excellent base while travelling to other cities around it. Grenoble is near the Alps so all year round you had an amazing view from the city of the mountains, whether it was 35℃ in Summer or snowwy in Winter. The seasonal changes means the city is bustling with different activities all year round, from skiing to water rafting. Not only does the city bring in a lot of tourists and skiers, but it is one of the cities in Europe with the largest student population - just over a third of the population is students!

Due to the wide range of people Grenoble attracts, it has many great bars and restaurants to accommodate everyone. After a few weeks in Grenoble, I realized that the city was great for eating out. Quai Mounier on the north side of the river Isère became our favorite place to go for a tasty meal. The whole street is full of pizzerias; it’s about fifteen restaurants in a row. Remember that people eat late in France, usually from 8.30-9.00pm.

During our first night out, we didn’t know anyone from Grenoble; however, we found everyone to be really friendly and helpful in recommending good places to go. A French girl suggested a discotheque where they danced Le rock, the French equivalence to jive. Definitely not my thing, but it’s popular on many discotheques in Grenoble.
